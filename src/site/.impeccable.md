## Design Context

### Users

Ludum Dare jam players — experienced game-jam browsers evaluating the entry in a
web embed, usually 5–15 minutes per session, often on a laptop at a desk late
at night during rating period. They arrive curious, impatient, and
over-exposed to game-jam aesthetics. They will bounce in seconds if the page
looks like "a website." They will stay if they can't tell, at first glance,
that they're in a game.

**Job to be done:** believe, for as long as possible, that they have
accidentally opened a real wartime newspaper — then discover, at their own
pace, that something is wrong with it.

### Brand Personality

The paper is *The Proper Goose*, and the tagline —
"Take a Gander at the daily News." — is the whole thesis.

Three words: **deadpan, wartime, unsettling.**

- **Voice**: scrupulously neutral broadsheet language; passive constructions;
  sources "who declined to be named because they were not authorized to speak."
  Never editorializes. The horror is always in what's omitted, sanitized, or
  quietly redefined.
- **Emotional goal**: creeping dread. The reader should feel at first that
  they're skimming a mildly stuffy newspaper, then notice, two or three
  articles in, that every piece is gently lying.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Period: World War II broadsheet** — late 1930s to mid 1940s. Think the
physical object: a single folded sheet of cheap newsprint, letterpress-set
metal type, register slightly off, ink bleeding into the paper fibres.

**Material over digital.** This is not "a website styled like a newspaper."
It is a newspaper that happens to render in a browser. Every decision
(typography, rules, column widths, whitespace, the way pull-quotes are set)
should be justifiable as something a 1942 compositor would have done at the
stone.

**Theme: light cream, loaded color.** Unbleached newsprint cream is
the paper ground; warm ink is the body type; above that sits a
**recruitment-poster chrome layer** — Flagg's *I Want YOU*, Liberty
Loan broadsides, "BUY WAR BONDS" drives, WPA labor posters. The
palette is sourced from the masthead seal and locked to four hues
besides ink and cream: ring red (`#B12330`), mustard (`#D8B246`),
burnt orange (`#D7821B`), wartime navy (`#1F3461`). No other colors
exist in this world.

**Application is loaded, not sober.** The register is a broadsheet
*carrying* a recruitment poster — rigorous news typography under
filled color banners, stamped kicker tabs, and gold-ground ornaments.
Body copy is still ink on cream; the *structural chrome* is inked in
poster color. Red fills section banners, EXTRA / STOP PRESS tabs,
the masthead date rule. Navy fills article kickers, byline
cartouches, nav-bar actives, masthead meta blocks. Gold fills
pull-quote grounds, ad-slot frames, the hero dropcap, ornamental
rules. At any moment ~30–40% of the visible page should read as
color, not ink — that is the point of difference from a sober
broadsheet.

**Anti-references** (explicitly NOT this):
- Modern NYT-redesign digital-first aesthetic — too clean, too 2020s, breaks
  the fiction instantly.
- Soviet/Constructivist propaganda — wrong country. The fiction is a
  US/UK-style home-front paper; Russian revolutionary red-and-black
  vocabulary (tilted sans-serif, geometric abstraction) reads as the
  wrong nation. Our register is Flagg, Rockwell, Leyendecker — not
  Rodchenko.
- Papers Please rubber-stamp / dithered aesthetic — right mood, wrong object.
  This is a broadsheet, not a document.
- Synthwave / neon / gradient-fill poster pastiches — the colors must
  feel *lithographed*, with flat fills and clean edges. No gradients,
  no glows, no drop shadows on type.
- Game-UI tropes: no buttons that look like buttons, no HUD, no menus, no
  "Start" screen. Navigation is nav bars, mastheads, datelines.

**Typographic direction** (to be executed in craft):
- Display face: a wartime-appropriate Modern / Scotch Roman serif with a
  strong vertical stress — period-accurate, not one of the design-Twitter
  reflex serifs. (Playfair Display currently in the repo is on the reflex
  reject list and must be replaced.)
- Body face: a solid, slightly squarish serif that survives being set small
  and tight, the way wartime newsprint had to.
- Decks and section labels: a condensed grotesque in small caps, or small
  caps of the display face — never a "technical" monospace.
- No icons. No logos other than the masthead nameplate. No emoji. Section
  markers are rules and labels set in type.

### Format & Viewport

This is the critical constraint that every other design decision flows
through. The game runs inside the Ludum Dare embedded player, not in an
open browser tab, and nearly everyone will rate it in that small frame.

**Primary target: 948 × 533 px** (the LD embed, ~16:9). Every layout
decision is made here first — the fold, the masthead, the hero article,
the visible-above-fold article count, the tap/click targets, the type
scale. If it doesn't look right at 948 × 533, it is broken.

**Secondary target: fullscreen / large desktop** (≥ 1280 wide). The game
should *also* be usable (and richer) here, but never designed here first.
At this size the paper gets more generous margins and a second column of
minor articles appears at the edge — never a wider hero or bigger
headline type. Broadsheets don't grow — they add columns.

**Not a target: mobile or tablet.** LD entries are rated on desktop. Do
not spend time on mobile layouts. A simple readable stacked fallback
below ~720 px wide is fine and no more than that.

**Vertical scroll is expected.** 533 px is the *fold*, not the canvas.
The paper can be a full broadsheet's worth of content, but the hero and
two secondary articles must be above the fold at 533 px. The masthead
may occupy up to ~90 px of vertical space; everything below fights for
the remaining ~440 px.

**Layout implications at 948 × 533:**

- `src-main` currently has `max-width: 1200px` — replace with a system
  that centers the sheet at ~912 px effective width inside the embed and
  scales up to ~1280 px max at fullscreen. The paper never goes wider
  than a real broadsheet (~1280 px at our chosen em scale).
- Masthead side-meta (volume number, date) stays inline with the
  nameplate at 948 wide — do not drop it to a second row. The three-part
  masthead is period-defining.
- Hero article takes the full column width above the fold, with a
  headline of ~2 decks (kicker + main) plus a dek. No image above the
  fold unless it is a small cut (≤ 140 px tall).
- Secondary articles run in a 3-column footer band below the fold.
- One rail column on the right (ad slot / standing column) appears only
  at ≥ 1100 px wide. Below that it drops into the main flow.

### Style Guidelines

Concrete tokens. These override the existing `global.css` where they
conflict.

**Typography**

- **Display** (masthead nameplate, headlines, decks):
  **Old Standard TT** — a Modern/Scotch Roman revival that is the closest
  free equivalent to the metal faces WWII-era broadsheets actually used
  (century-expanded / miller / scotch family). Sits right for 1942.
  Playfair Display is on the reflex-reject list and is also
  stylistically wrong (too fashion-magazine) — remove it from
  `index.html`'s font link.
- **Body** (article copy, decks at small sizes, bylines):
  **Gelasio** — Cyrus Highsmith's news-serif, designed explicitly for
  newspaper body copy at small sizes. Holds up at 14 px. If adding
  Gelasio feels like scope creep mid-jam, keep **Source Serif 4** —
  it's on-disk already and off the reject list.
- **Condensed sans**: remove **Oswald** entirely. It is modern,
  anachronistic, and overused. Section rules and running heads use
  letterspaced small caps of Old Standard TT instead.
- Update the Google Fonts link in `index.html` to load
  `Old+Standard+TT:ital,wght@0,400;0,700;1,400` plus `Gelasio` (if
  adopted), and drop Playfair + Oswald.

**Type scale** (tuned for 948 × 533, not the current 17 px base):

```
--text-xxs:  0.6875rem;  /* 11px — running heads, microcopy */
--text-xs:   0.75rem;    /* 12px — bylines, datelines, eyebrows */
--text-sm:   0.8125rem;  /* 13px — small caps decks, captions */
--text-base: 0.9375rem;  /* 15px — body copy; 14px on small viewports */
--text-md:   1.0625rem;  /* 17px — sub-hero headlines */
--text-lg:   1.375rem;   /* 22px — secondary headlines */
--text-xl:   1.75rem;    /* 28px — front-page hero dek */
--text-2xl:  2.375rem;   /* 38px — hero headline */
--text-3xl:  3rem;       /* 48px — masthead nameplate */
```

Base font size: **15 px** at ≥ 948 wide; **14 px** below. No fluid
clamp on body copy — newspapers set at a fixed size. Fluid clamp is
permitted on the masthead nameplate only.

**Rule weights** (these are the bones of a broadsheet — get them right):

```
--rule-hairline: 0.5px;  /* column dividers between article blocks */
--rule-thin:     1px;    /* section separators */
--rule-thick:    2px;    /* front-page top rule above hero */
--rule-double:   3px double; /* masthead underline — period-defining */
```

**Spacing** (tighter than current — broadsheet rhythm, not web rhythm):

```
--space-1: 0.25rem;   /*  4px */
--space-2: 0.375rem;  /*  6px */
--space-3: 0.5rem;    /*  8px */
--space-4: 0.75rem;   /* 12px */
--space-5: 1rem;      /* 16px — default gutter */
--space-6: 1.5rem;    /* 24px — section break */
--space-7: 2rem;      /* 32px */
--space-8: 3rem;      /* 48px — top-of-page only */
```

Column gutters: **16 px** at primary viewport, **24 px** at fullscreen.
Never larger. Wide gutters modernize the paper instantly.

**Color** — camp-poster palette sourced from the masthead seal,
expressed in OKLCH. Canonical source of truth lives in
`styles/global.css`.

```
--paper-base:  oklch(0.95 0.012 100); /* pulp cream — #F3F3E8 */

--ink-base:    oklch(0.18 0.01 40);   /* warm near-black, never #000 */
--ink-soft:    oklch(0.35 0.01 40);   /* bylines, datelines */
--ink-muted:   oklch(0.48 0.008 40);  /* captions, microcopy */
--ink-rule:    oklch(0.78 0.015 90);  /* hairline rules */
--ink-strong:  oklch(0.10 0.01 40);   /* top rules, nameplate */

--accent:      oklch(0.49 0.19 25);   /* ring red — #B12330 */
--gold:        oklch(0.78 0.13 85);   /* mustard — #D8B246 */
--gold-deep:   oklch(0.66 0.17 55);   /* burnt orange — #D7821B */
--navy:        oklch(0.32 0.09 258);  /* wartime navy — #1F3461 */
--navy-deep:   oklch(0.25 0.10 258);  /* deep navy — pull-quote attribution */
```

**Application — where each color lives.** Every color has a named
slot. Unassigned decoration is forbidden.

| Color | Slots |
|-------|-------|
| `--color-accent` (red) | Section banners, EXTRA / STOP PRESS tabs, masthead date rule, hero kicker bar. |
| `--color-navy` | Article kickers, byline cartouches, nav active state, masthead volume/date blocks, pull-quote attribution. |
| `--color-gold` | Pull-quote grounds, ad-slot frames, hero dropcap ground, ornamental rules, WAR BONDS-style CTAs. |
| `--color-ink` | Body copy, headlines, captions, taglines — everything downstream of the chrome. |
| `--color-paper` | Page ground, article cards, fine gutters. |

What color *cannot* do, regardless of slot: body text, hover color,
button fill for its own sake, random decoration, gradients, glows.
Colored grounds always carry cream or warm-ink type at AA contrast —
never a washed gray. Pure black and pure white remain banned.

**Realism details** (what makes this read as a real 1940s paper vs. a
pastiche):

1. **Misregistered color fills.** Poster-print color is *always* very
   slightly off its stroke — the lithograph tell. Implement two ways:
   at the ribbon / banner scale, nudge the text `translateY(0.5px)`
   inside the filled ground. At the eyebrow / tab scale, achieve the
   same effect with **asymmetric vertical padding** (e.g., `2px top,
   1px bottom`) so the type sits off-center on its ground. Every
   filled poster-color chrome element should carry the tell one way
   or the other.
2. **Column rules, not card dividers.** Multi-column article lists are
   separated by a 0.5 px hairline vertical rule, never by gaps or
   cards.
3. **Justified body copy with hyphenation.** Set `text-align: justify`
   and `hyphens: auto` on article body paragraphs. Wartime papers were
   always justified.
4. **Dropcap on hero sits on a gold ground.** 3-line dropcap set in
   the display face, reversed out on a flat gold tile — WPA-poster
   initial, not a NYT blackletter initial.
5. **Italic strap-lines / tag-lines.** The masthead tagline and section
   kickers sit in italic body text, never bold sans.
6. **Rag-right is forbidden outside pull-quotes.** Pull-quotes *can*
   rag right because they were typically hand-set as display elements.
7. **Typographic marks only.** Any glyph the layout needs (§, ¶, ★, ·,
   —) is set in type, not drawn. No emoji, no icons, no SVG symbols.
   The favicon seal is the single exception and never appears in-page.
8. **Dateline every article.** `CAPITAL CITY, Apr. 1 — ` opens the
   body copy in small caps followed by an em dash, period-accurate.
9. **All-caps on colored ground is fine; all-caps in body text is
   banned.** Filled red and navy tabs carry letterspaced all-caps
   sans-weight display type; body text uses sentence case.
10. **Numerals are old-style figures** where available (`font-feature-
    settings: "onum"` on body, lining figures on decks and on colored
    chrome where they need to punch).
11. **Colored chrome stacks flat.** No drop shadow, no gradient, no
    inner glow. Every fill is a single solid hue with a crisp edge.
    Poster ink does not blur.

### Design Principles

1. **The UI never winks.** No element should wave at the player.
   Everything looks routine — in a 1942 recruitment-poster sense of
   "routine", where a red EXTRA banner above the fold is *normal*.
   The reader supplies the dread.

2. **Material over screen.** Prefer rules, columns, flat color fills, and
   type hierarchy over cards, shadows, and rounded corners. No drop
   shadows, no glassmorphism, no modals, no gradients. If a 1942
   lithograph couldn't have printed it, neither do we.

3. **Hierarchy by rank *and* chrome.** The hero dominates because it's
   bigger, better-placed, *and* sits under a red banner with a gold
   dropcap. Broadsheet composition (column rules, multi-deck headlines,
   dateline) plus poster chrome (filled banners, stamped kickers,
   colored grounds). Both layers do work; neither is optional.

4. **Loaded color, disciplined slots.** Every swatch sits in a named
   slot from the application table. Red = banners, stamps, date rule.
   Navy = kickers, cartouches, meta. Gold = grounds, ornaments, CTAs.
   What color never does: body text, hover color, random decoration,
   gradients, glows. The discipline is in the *where*, not the *how
   much*.

5. **Drift is the mechanic.** Visual state is a first-class narrative
   surface. Design tokens should be swappable — ink tone, cream tone, rule
   weight, accent saturation, leading — so `state.js` can nudge them as the
   game advances without re-authoring components.

6. **Accessibility is non-negotiable in a jam build.** WCAG AA contrast on
   body text at every drift state, and on every colored chrome fill —
   cream on red, cream on navy, ink on gold all need to hit AA.
   Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` — any animation registered with
   `fx/` must degrade gracefully via the `opts.reducedMotion` flag,
   preserving narrative beats without vestibular risk.
